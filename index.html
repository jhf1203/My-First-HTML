I hate to brag, but...

I'm a pretty good sandwich maker. Being a father of a very active 1-year-old I've learned to make the most out of every second of free time that I have, and have thus learned how to make a bomb Turkey Sandwich in under five minutes. Want to learn?

  1. Retrieve the following things from the fridge: Turkey, a stick of butter, dijon mustard lettuce, and bacon.
  2. Retrieve the following things from the spice rack: Garlic Powder and Herbes De Provence
  3. Remove two slices of sourdough bread from the bag
  4. cut two slices of butter from the stick, and then quarter each slice.
  5. Place each quarter in a "quadrant" of one side of each piece of bread
  6. Place bread in the microwave and cook for approximately 30 seconds to soften butter
  7. spread the butter liberally so that both pieces of bread are appropriately slathered on their appropriate side
  8. Sprinkle the garlic powder and herbes de provence onto the buttered side of the bread to your liking
  9. Place the butter in the toaster oven butter side up, set to broil
  10. Remove two pieces of bacon from the packaging, place on a plate, and microwave for three minutes (+/- 30 seconds for thickness)
  11. Remove the bacon from the microwave and set the plate aside to cool
  12. Remove the bread from the toaster over.
  13. Open the cap on the dijon mustard and spread the desired amount on the non-buttered side of each bread slice
  14. Tear each bacon strip in half (the short way) and place two of each half-size strip on the mustard side of the bread
  15. Place your desired amount of turkey on top of the bacon on either piece of bread you choose
  16. Place your desired amount of lettuce (I like two pieces of green leaf lettuce) on top of the turkey
  17. One piece of bread should only have mustard and bacon showing. Place this on top of the lettuce on the other bread slice so that the bacon is touching the lettuce
  18. Place this on the plate you used to cook the bacon, to reduce dish usage, and enjoy!
